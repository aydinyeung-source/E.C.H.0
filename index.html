    How to Play

    You are somewhere you shouldn't be: an endless dark maze that is already inhabited. You cannot fight anything here. You survive by seeing without being seen, and by knowing when to stand still in the dark.

    The goal

    Explore. Your score is the number of distinct cells you set foot in — so wandering into new corridors is everything, and pacing the same ground counts for nothing. There is no finish line. You go until something catches you.

    Seeing — the sonar

    The world is pitch black. Send Sonar fires a ring that lights up every surface it sweeps over, which then fades back to black over about fifteen seconds — like glow-in-the-dark.

    • It costs energy, and it has a 17.5-second cooldown. The button (and the radar ring) fills back up as it recharges. You get one look, and then a couple of seconds of true darkness before the next.
    • The radar, bottom-right, maps the walls around you when you ping — through walls and round corners. It's a proximity sense, not a map of the level. Cyan marks a gap you can fit through.
    • A ping is loud. Everything within earshot comes to look — even things that had given up on you. And for three seconds after, anything with a clear line to you can see you at any distance. Fire from behind a corner, never standing in the open.

    Being seen — the most important rule

    There are two ways the inhabitants find you, and the second is the one that kills:

    • Up close. Within about two and a half cells they can pick you out of the dark on their own — behind you, round a corner, anywhere.
    • Eye contact. If one is on your screen and it is facing you, it sees you — at any distance whatsoever. A thing wandering across a junction with its back turned is safe to watch. The moment it turns towards you while you're looking at it, you've locked eyes, and it is coming.

    And if you hold that eye contact for more than two seconds, it fixates: ten seconds of pursuit that tracks you through walls and cannot be shaken or waited out. Only a crucifix ends it.

    So the golden rule is: look away. Watching a distant thing is how it gets you.

    The inhabitants

    They were here before you. Left alone they wander their own routes. Once one is genuinely hunting you it moves faster than your walk — but slower than your sprint, so you can always outrun it in a straight line.

    • Chase it long enough without being caught and it gives up, wandering off and ignoring you for a while. Use that quiet.
    • The danger meter, top-left, fills as the nearest one closes in. It never tells you which direction — only that it's getting worse.
    • Their eyes give off a faint red glow. Sometimes that's your only warning.
    • One that catches you dies doing it — small comfort, but the maze is quieter for it.

    Staying alive

    • Energy drains while you run and when you ping. It comes back when you slow down — a little while walking, fastest of all when you stand completely still. Run all the way out and you're locked to a walk until you've caught your breath.
    • Meat lying in the maze restores energy. Stacks deep.
    • Torch — a narrow beam that lights the way ahead, but shine it at an entity and you enrage it. Each torch is one full burn.
    • Crucifix — your panic button, and rare. It blinds every entity in the maze at once, wipes their memory of you entirely, and gives you a burst of speed. The only thing that ends a fixation. Carry it for the moment you truly need it. You'll also find it in two halves — a top and a bottom. Carry both, select the top half and press USE: over 3 seconds, for a little energy, the two fit together into a whole one.
    • Broken windows — a smashed hole in a wall you can vault through and they cannot follow. They can still see you through it, so it buys distance, not escape.

    Safe rooms

    Now and then the halls open into a sealed room with a heavy door. It's the one place you're truly safe — and getting safe is what makes you unsafe.

    1. A ten-digit code is painted on the door. Read it, and remember it — the keypad's screen covers the door while you type, so you enter it blind. You can leave and come back; it keeps what you've entered.
    2. Enter the code, then shove the door open with your body and step inside. The door is deafening — everything within two chunks comes to the doorway — but it holds against anything. What you've bought is a crowd outside the only way out.
    3. Finish the terminal task and the things outside scatter, and the locker opens with a reward inside.
    4. The door only works once: walk back out and it never shuts again.
    5. The vent in the back wall is a second way out that only you fit through — but pry it open and it can't be closed, and it's how they'll get in. Use it to leave, not to hide.

    Seeds & the daily challenge

    Every maze comes from a seed — the same seed is the same maze, everywhere, for everyone. The Daily Challenge is one shared seed per day; how far you explore it goes on the leaderboard.
